# FOW Interest Management Benchmark — 2026-03-28_21h54 — commit 5120e71

**Goal:** Prove that VisibilityTracker + SpatialGrid filter cross-group
entities when two groups of bots are placed beyond DEFAULT_VISION_RANGE=150u.

## Spawn Layouts

| Mode    | Zone A                  | Zone B                 | Min distance |
|---------|-------------------------|------------------------|--------------|
| cluster | x∈[-80,80] y∈[-80,80]  | same zone              | 0u           |
| bimodal | x∈[-400,-200] y∈[-100,100] | x∈[200,400] y∈[-100,100] | ~400u   |

Vision range = 150u → bimodal groups are completely invisible to each other.

## Results

| Scenario                     | Connected   | Avg Tick  | Out          | In           | Delta Eff.   |
|------------------------------|-------------|-----------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| Cluster (FOW=OFF baseline)   | 38/40       | 0.33ms    | 3165.4kbps   | 349.5kbps    | 73%          |
| Bimodal (FOW=ON groups)      | 40/40       | 0.29ms    | 2710.5kbps   | 357.9kbps    | 70%          |

## FOW Effectiveness

- Out_bimodal / Out_cluster = **85.6%**
- Bandwidth reduction attributed to FOW = **14.4%**
- Target: ~50% (each bot only receives its group's 40/2 peers)

## Notes

- **Cluster baseline**: all bots within ±80u — FOW has no effect; every bot
  sees every other bot → N² updates → maximum bandwidth.
- **Bimodal FOW**: 50% of bots in Zone A (x≈-300), 50% in Zone B (x≈+300).
  Distance between zones ≈ 400-600u >> vision range 150u.
  VisibilityTracker detects no re-entries after initial spawn; SpatialGrid
  never marks opposing-zone cells visible → zero cross-group state updates.
- **Delta Efficiency**: higher in bimodal expected if bots move less (stay
  within zone) but delta compression itself is orthogonal to FOW filtering.
